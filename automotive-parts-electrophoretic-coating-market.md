<p><strong>The Automotive Parts Electrophoretic Coating Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>"Cathodic Electrophoretic Coating"</li><li>"Anodic Electrophoretic Coating"</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive parts electrophoretic coating market is primarily divided into two types: cathodic and anodic electrophoretic coatings. Cathodic electrophoretic coating (CEC) involves the deposition of negatively charged particles onto positively charged substrates, providing enhanced corrosion resistance and durability. In contrast, anodic electrophoretic coating (AEC) uses positively charged particles, which can offer better aesthetic finishes but typically less corrosion protection. Both types are used for automotive parts to improve surface quality and longevity while contributing to overall vehicle performance and appearance.</p></p>

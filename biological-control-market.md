<p><p>Biological control markets encompass various strategies to manage pests using natural predators. Predatory mites target pests like spider mites and thrips, enhancing crop health. Insects, such as ladybugs and parasitic wasps, control aphid populations and other harmful insects. Nematodes are microscopic worms that prey on soil-dwelling pests, benefiting root systems. Other biological control methods include traps, pathogens, and fungi that manage pest populations. Together, these methods promote sustainable agriculture by reducing reliance on chemical pesticides.</p></p>

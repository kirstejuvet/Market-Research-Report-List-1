<p><p>The liquid helium temperature zone cryogenic refrigerator market includes two primary types: Gifford-McMahon refrigerators and pulse tube refrigerators. Gifford-McMahon refrigerators utilize a mechanical cycle that compresses and expands gas to achieve low temperatures, making them efficient for cryogenic applications. Pulse tube refrigerators, on the other hand, use a gas oscillation technique, effectively eliminating moving parts in the cold region, which minimizes maintenance and enhances reliability. Both technologies are vital for applications in research, medical imaging, and materials science.</p></p>

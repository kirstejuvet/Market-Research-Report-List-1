<p><p>A Single Point Mooring (SPM) System is a buoy mooring system that allows a tanker to moor at a single point offshore, facilitating the loading and unloading of oil and gas. This system is commonly used in deepwater ports and offshore terminals, providing flexibility and safety by minimizing vessel movement. <p><ul><li>Drag Embedment Anchors (DEA)</li><li>Vertical Load Anchors (VLA)</li><li>Suction Anchors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Single Point Mooring (SPM) system market includes various anchoring technologies such as Drag Embedment Anchors (DEA), Vertical Load Anchors (VLA), and Suction Anchors. DEAs are designed to embed into the seafloor through drag forces, providing stable anchorage. VLAs utilize vertical loads to enhance stability and are effective in deep-water applications. Suction Anchors create a vacuum to penetrate the seabed, offering strong holding capacity. Each type addresses specific marine conditions and operational requirements for floating platforms.</p></p>

<p><p>Anti-reflective glass coatings are designed to minimize reflections and enhance light transmission in various applications. Two prominent methods for applying these coatings are electron beam evaporation and sputtering. Electron beam evaporation involves heating materials to create a vapor that condenses on the glass, producing a uniform and durable coating. Sputtering, on the other hand, uses energetic particles to dislodge atoms from a target material, which then deposit onto the glass surface. Both techniques improve optical clarity and reduce glare, making them essential in various industries.</p></p>
